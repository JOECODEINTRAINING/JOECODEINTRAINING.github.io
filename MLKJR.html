Martin Luther King Jr.

1) Martin Luther King Jr. was an American Baptist minister and activist who became the most visible spokesperson and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968. He is best known for advancing civil rights using nonviolent civil disobedience based on his Christian beliefs.

2) Even after renouncing personal use of guns, King had a complex relationship with self-defense in the movement. He publicly discouraged it as a widespread practice but acknowledged that it was sometimes necessary.[368] Throughout his career King was frequently protected by other civil rights activists who carried arms, such as Colonel Stone Johnson,[369] Robert Hayling, and the Deacons for Defense and Justice.[370][371]
